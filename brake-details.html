Project information

  • Category: Tooling

Custom Brake tool

Problems Involved

  • 4000+ units for one part outsourced yearly
  • Brackets couldn't fit in the brake with the available tooling
  • Direction of grain boundary affects several factors like tonnage required, springback, e.t.c
  • Excess springback would affect part alignment as the central axis of the slots/holes would no longer be coincident

Solutions Applied

  • Designed a tool to be installed on the TRIUMPF TruBend 5130 to bend the metal brackets with air bending
  • Used 3D printing and sheet metal stacking for initial testing to drive design changes
  • Used parametric dimensions for the design so that changing 3 variables(original length,thickness & base width) was enough to make a new tool for a new part
  • A suitable springback factor that will keep the product within the assigned tolerance in both cases of beig along the grain and against the grain was selected
  • Final product was machined from 4130 tool steel to ensure durability(scracthes on the brackets were not a conccern since it gets sandblasted and painted before assembly)

End Results

  • Tool was able to bend the part to the required angle without damaging the part
  • Multiple tools could be stacked alongside each other to bend multiple parts at once
  • Final design could be adapted for 3 other parts that were formerly outsourced
