Torsional testing

Problems Involved

  • Old Torionsal testing rig tends to tilt whenever a load greater than 150lbs is applied upwards

Solutions Applied

  • We cut slots in the testing rig and bolted it down to a concrete floor to fix it in place
  • We replaced the shocks with solid tubes to ensure accurate readings
  • We applied a load at the front right side with a crane scale. The load was progressively increased upto twice the weight applied by the front right wheel to the ground
  • We attached laser pointers to joints on the car we were concerned about with 3D printed fixtures and pointed them at a whiteboard. We then measured the vertical displacement and used some trig to find the angle of twist at each joint

End Results

  • We discovered that the chassis has a torsional rigidity of 2620 lb-ft/deg
  • We noticed a huge disparity between the test and the FEA model in the rear section of the vehicle. After some investigation, we found that the rear section of the chassis had some extra tubes added to comply with competition rules and these tubes ere not added to the CAD model.
